Transportation in Rome

Buses

Rome has currently over 350 bus lines and it can sometimes be a bit confusing to find the right bus.
Find out the main bus lines in Rome,
their schedules and frequencies and tips to help you get around the city on this type of public transport.


Types of buses In Rome there are various types of lines, these are the most popular:



  • Urban lines (U): The large majority. They start between 5 am and 6:30 am and finish at midnight.

  • Night buses (N): These lines operate while the urban lines "rest", that is to say, between midnight and 5:00-6:00 am.

  • Express (X): These lines are for the outskirts of Rome, for longer journeys.

  • Exact (E): These lines link the centre with the surrounding neighbourhoods. They run on fixed timetables.

    • Tram

    Rome has a tram network consisting of six lines that serve the city and the surrounding areas. The tram system is operated by ATAC, the public transport company of Rome.

    Here is some general information on trams in Rome:

    Lines: Rome's tram network consists of six lines: 2, 3, 5, 8, 14, and 19.

    Hours of operation: Trams generally operate from 5:30 AM until midnight, although some lines may have slightly different hours.

    Fares: The same ticket used for buses and the metro can also be used on trams. Single tickets can be purchased from vending machines located at tram stops, or from tobacconists, newsstands, and other authorized retailers.

    Frequency: The frequency of trams varies depending on the line and the time of day, but generally they run every 8-12 minutes during peak hours and every 15-20 minutes during off-peak hours.

    Interchanges: Trams in Rome connect to other forms of public transport, including the metro and buses, at various points throughout the city.

    Route maps: Maps of the tram routes can be found at tram stops and on the ATAC website.

    History: Rome's tram system has a long history, dating back to the late 19th century. The current network includes both modern trams and historic tramcars that have been restored for use on certain routes.

    Metro

    Rome has a metro system that consists of three lines: Line A (orange), Line B (blue), and Line C (green). The metro system is operated by ATAC, the public transport company of Rome.

    Here is some general information on the metro in Rome:

    Lines: Rome's metro system consists of three lines: Line A (orange), Line B (blue), and Line C (green).

    Hours of operation: The metro generally operates from 5:30 AM until midnight, although some stations may have slightly different hours.

    Fares: The same ticket used for buses and trams can also be used on the metro. Single tickets can be purchased from vending machines located at metro stations, or from tobacconists, newsstands, and other authorized retailers.

    Frequency: The frequency of trains varies depending on the line and the time of day, but generally they run every 5-10 minutes during peak hours and every 10-15 minutes during off-peak hours.

    Interchanges: The metro in Rome connects to other forms of public transport, including buses and trams, at various points throughout the city.

    Route maps: Maps of the metro routes can be found at metro stations and on the ATAC website.

    History: Rome's metro system has a relatively short history, with Line A opening in 1980 and Line B in 1990. Line C, which is still being expanded, began operation in 2014.

    Taxis

    Taxis are a convenient and relatively affordable way to get around Rome. They are easily recognizable by their white color and "TAXI" sign on the roof. Here is some general information on taxis in Rome:

    Taxi stands: Taxis can be found at designated taxi stands throughout the city, which are marked by a "TAXI" sign. They can also be hailed on the street, although this is less common.

    Fares: Taxis in Rome are regulated by the city, and fares are calculated based on distance and time. There is a base fee for entering the taxi, and then a per-kilometer rate that increases at night and on weekends. There may also be additional fees for luggage, airport transfers, and other special circumstances.

    Payment: Taxis in Rome generally accept both cash and credit card payments. However, it is always a good idea to confirm with the driver before starting the ride.

    Taxi apps: There are several taxi apps available in Rome, such as MyTaxi and Uber, which allow users to hail and pay for taxis using their smartphones. These apps can be especially useful for getting a taxi during peak hours or in areas with few taxi stands.

    Safety: Taxis in Rome are generally safe and reliable. However, it is always a good idea to use licensed taxis and to be cautious when taking a taxi alone at night.
    Tips: Tipping is not mandatory in Italy, but it is common to round up the fare or add a small tip for good service.
